My DM verdict

Right now the game feels like:

Strong atmosphere + clear stakes

But the decision-space compresses under pressure (lots of “any choice escalates”), and when the story hits high danger it often becomes a funnel toward death unless you pick the one “correct” vibe.

The 3 highest-impact tweaks (based on these 10 runs)

Add a “pressure release valve” once danger hits alto
At “alto”, ensure at least one option (or a discovered tool/ally) can reduce danger back to “medio” if the player plays smart.

Make inventory matter in options + consequences
If the player has a linterna/daga/poción, it should reliably unlock a different option or soften a consequence (even if it’s not always optimal).

Anti-loop rule for repeated actions
If the player tries the same category twice (escape/force/stonewall), the next turn should change the scene (new NPC arrives, location changes, external timer triggers, etc.) rather than repeating “they get angrier and pull weapon again.”
